2026 CMS Care Management Program Selector

Care Management programs let teams bill Medicare for structured coordination and ongoing support for patients with chronic or acute needs. These codes support remote monitoring, behavioral health management, transition support, and primary care under the CY 2026 Physician Fee Schedule.
What is RPM?
Remote Physiologic Monitoring (RPM) uses connected digital devices to collect patient vitals outside of office visits. It enables management of chronic/acute conditions, with new 2026 codes supporting shorter time increments and more flexible monitoring days.
Patient Eligibility
- Medicare patients with chronic or acute conditions
- Device supplied by care team, with consent
- Required number of transmission days (2-15 for 99445, 16+ for 99454)
- Not duplicative with other management codes.
Who May Bill?
RPM: Physicians, NPs, PAs, clinical staff under general supervision. Only one provider per month per patient. Device supply must be billed by furnishing entity; IDTFs excluded. Provider/QHP documentation required.
Billing Structure
RPM supports:
- One-time setup
- Monthly device supply/review
- Add-ons for addl. minutes
- Codes for 2–15 or 16–30 days and 10–19 min engagement.
Workflow
1
Patient Onboarding
Consent, device setup, education
2
Data Collection
Daily device use, transmission (min. 2–16 days/mo)
3
Care Management
Provider review (10–30+ min), ongoing documentation
4
Escalation/Follow-up
Referral or care changes for flagged results

What is RTM?
Remote Therapeutic Monitoring (RTM) tracks non-physiologic data (e.g., medication adherence, symptom diaries, therapy device use) using digital tools for patients with musculoskeletal or respiratory conditions. New 2026 codes expand supply and management options.
Patient Eligibility
- Medicare patients with MSK or respiratory conditions
- Device or app prescribed
- Transmission minimums (2–15 days for new code, 16+ for legacy)
- Not duplicate to other programs.
Who May Bill?
Physicians, QHPs, therapists. Device can be supplied by the organization or contract vendor in compliance with CMS rules.
Billing Structure
- Setup/education (98975)
- Monthly device (98976/77 or new unified supply)
- Management: initial (98980) + add-on (98981)
- New 2026 duration supply/short engagement codes.
Workflow
1
Onboarding
Device/app setup for therapy tracking
2
Tracking Period
Symptom/events, activity, med log (2–30 days)
3
Review & Support
Provider review 10–20+ min, doc, escalation as indicated

What is CCM?
Chronic Care Management (CCM) supports patients with multiple chronic conditions requiring regular monitoring, medication management, and care coordination across providers. Services are structured and non-face-to-face with required documentation.
Patient Eligibility
- Medicare beneficiaries with at least 2 chronic conditions expected to last 12+ months
- Significant risk of death, acute exacerbation, or functional decline
- Comprehensive care plan documented and shared with the patient.
Who May Bill?
Physicians, NPs, PAs, and clinical staff under general supervision. Only one practitioner per patient per month. Staff time billed via “incident to.”
Billing Structure
- Monthly management (99490)
- Complex CCM (99487, add-on 99489)
- Add-ons for additional 20 min (99439)
- Psychiatric Collaborative Care options also available.
Workflow
1
Enrollment
Patient identified, consent, care plan written
2
Monthly Management
Non-face-to-face team follow-up, med refills/edits, status checks
3
Care Plan Review/Escalation
Review progress, adjust plan, trigger in-person if needed

What is PCM?
Principal Care Management (PCM) is designed for Medicare patients with a single high-risk or complex chronic condition requiring monthly management. PCM codes support focused condition management and coordination.
Patient Eligibility
- Medicare beneficiaries with one high-risk chronic condition
- Condition must be expected to last at least 3+ months and place the patient at risk of hospitalization or functional decline.
Who May Bill?
Physicians, NPs, PAs, and clinical staff (incident to). Only one PCM biller per patient per month.
Billing Structure
- Monthly base management by clinical staff or provider (99426)
- Add-on code for each additional 30 min (99427)
Workflow
1
Assessment & Enrollment
Identify high-risk patient, document need, consent.
2
Monthly PCM
Non-face-to-face interventions, med/symptom tracking, plan updates.
3
Care Review
Escalate to CCM, RPM, or TCM as health status changes.

What is TCM?
Transitional Care Management (TCM) supports the transition of Medicare patients from hospital/institutional care back to community. TCM addresses medication reconciliation, follow-up visits, and coordination within 7–14 days post-discharge.
Patient Eligibility
- Medicare beneficiaries discharged from inpatient, observation, SNF, or partial hospitalization
- Requires moderate or high medical decision complexity.
Who May Bill?
Physicians, NPs, PAs. Only one TCM episode per patient per discharge event, not concurrent with CCM/PCM the same month.
Billing Structure
- Single face-to-face visit within 7 (99496) or 14 (99495) days
- Includes pre-visit outreach, med reconciliation, and ongoing review for 30 days after discharge
Workflow
1
Outreach/Discharge
Contact patient, review drug/med list.
2
F2F Visit
Schedule, document visit within timeframe (7 or 14 days).
3
Care Coordination
Monitor status, referrals, escalate as necessary.

What is APCM?
Advanced Primary Care Management (APCM) covers coordinated, team-based longitudinal primary care for high-risk Medicare populations, supporting chronic disease, preventive services, and care transitions via enhanced codes and payment pathways under 2026 CMS final rule.
Patient Eligibility
- Medicare patients attributed to an advanced primary care management program
- High risk or complex needs as defined by CMS
- Comprehensive care plan and team-based follow-up required.
Who May Bill?
Primary care physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and team members meeting supervision requirement in APM/ACO framework. FQHC/RHC-specific “G” codes may be used for these services.
Billing Structure
- Monthly base payment (G0511 for FQHC/RHC)
- Enhanced “G” codes for complex navigation and social determinant support (G0019, G0022, G0023, G0024, G0140, etc.)
- Add-on codes for additional time/services per rules.
Workflow
1
Team Assignment
Patient assigned to care management team, risk stratified
2
Monthly Engagement
Care plan development, outreach, ongoing team follow-up
3
SDOH/Complex Needs
SDOH screening, peer navigation, interdisciplinary consults, escalation as indicated
