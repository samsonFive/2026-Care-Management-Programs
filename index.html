2026 CMS Care Management Program Selector

Care Management programs let teams bill Medicare for structured coordination and ongoing support for patients with chronic or acute needs. These codes support remote monitoring, behavioral health management, transition support, and primary care under the CY 2026 Physician Fee Schedule.
CY 2026 Care Management Overview

Beginning January 1, 2026, Medicare finalized a broad set of updates to care management under the Physician Fee Schedule. Time‑based services such as RPM, RTM, CCM, behavioral health integration, and other care management codes are exempt from the new “efficiency adjustment” that reduces work RVUs for many non‑time‑based services, which helps protect longitudinal care revenues even as other services see downward pressure.[web:29][web:147][web:157]

CMS also clarified that services designated as care management will be treated as care coordination services for FQHCs and RHCs, aligning payment policy across settings and tying many of these services into the general care management code G0511.[web:29][web:149] In parallel, virtual direct supervision and expanded telehealth flexibilities—including removal of certain visit frequency limits—were made permanent, which supports remote care teams and digital‑first workflows.[web:147][web:150]

For remote care, 2026 introduces new short‑duration RPM and RTM codes for partial‑month engagement, higher rates for core management codes, and clearer stacking rules with CCM and other longitudinal programs, signaling CMS’s shift toward a more integrated, tech‑enabled care management ecosystem.[web:148][web:150][web:159]

Integrated Behavioral Health: Payment & Models

High-level reference for General BHI (99484), CoCM (99492–99494), HCPCS G0323, and APCM BH add-on codes (G0568–G0570): what they pay, who can bill, and which validated tools and assessments are required.

Program Stacking & Overlap Rules

Device programs (RPM/RTM), longitudinal care programs (CCM/PCM/APCM), transitional care (TCM), and behavioral health (BHI/CoCM) can often be billed together in the same month, but only when each program’s requirements are met independently, time is not double-counted, and CMS stacking exclusions are enforced.

Program Selection Decision Guide

Start by asking whether the clinic is participating in APCM. If yes, APCM becomes the base longitudinal program and you layer RPM/RTM, BHI/CoCM, and TCM as add-ons. If not, route into CCM (for 2+ chronic conditions) or PCM (for one serious condition), then add remote monitoring and behavioral health based on clinical need and team capacity.

Choosing Between BHI and CoCM

General BHI is a lower-intensity integration model (≥20 minutes/month) that can stand alone and fits mild–moderate behavioral health needs. CoCM is a higher-intensity model (60–70+ minutes/month) that requires a three-person team and registry-based case management, reserved for moderate–severe or complex behavioral health conditions.

Continuation Standards: BHI & CoCM

Apply continuation standards monthly so that programs match real clinical need: BHI continues while there is an active BH condition, validated scales in use, a living care plan, and ≥20 minutes of documented work; CoCM continues only while the full team and registry are active and 60–70+ minutes of care manager time remain necessary.

Patient Archetypes & Optimal Program Mix

Use these archetypes to design playbooks and pro formas: complex primary care patient (APCM + RPM + CoCM), multi-morbid patient (complex CCM + RPM + BHI), single high-risk condition (PCM + RTM + BHI), mild BH needs (CCM regular + RPM + BHI), and severe BH needs (complex CCM + RPM + CoCM).

Team Requirements by Program

Supports staffing decisions by clarifying required vs optional roles across programs: RPM (billing provider + clinical staff + device specialist), RTM (billing provider/therapist + therapy staff), CCM/PCM (billing provider + clinical staff), APCM (multidisciplinary primary care team), TCM (billing provider + clinical staff + case manager), BHI (billing provider + clinical staff), and CoCM (required triad of provider, BH care manager, psychiatric consultant).

CCM Service Levels: Regular vs Complex

Regular CCM fits patients with 2+ chronic conditions, 20–40 minutes of monthly non-face-to-face work, and low–moderate medical decision making. Complex CCM is appropriate when you consistently spend ≥60 minutes and decision-making is moderate–high with multiple specialists, frequent exacerbations, and high coordination needs.

Platform Integration & Roadmap

  • Use APCM as the base where available; do not stack CCM/PCM/TCM on top of APCM months.
  • Layer RPM or RTM (choose one) whenever remote monitoring adds clinical value.
  • Choose between regular and complex CCM based on documented time and decision complexity.
  • Start with BHI for broad BH integration; reserve CoCM for higher-acuity patients and only when the full team and registry are in place.
