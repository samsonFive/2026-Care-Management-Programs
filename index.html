Care Management programs let teams bill Medicare for structured coordination and ongoing support for patients with chronic or acute needs. These codes support remote monitoring, behavioral health management, transition support, and primary care under the CY 2026 Physician Fee Schedule.
What is RPM?
Remote Physiologic Monitoring (RPM) uses connected digital devices to collect patient vitals outside of office visits. It enables management of chronic/acute conditions, with new 2026 codes supporting shorter time increments and more flexible monitoring days.
Patient Eligibility
- Medicare patients with chronic or acute conditions
- Device supplied by care team, with consent
- Required number of transmission days (2-15 for 99445, 16+ for 99454)
- Not duplicative with other management codes.
Who May Bill?
RPM: Physicians, NPs, PAs, clinical staff under general supervision. Only one provider per month per patient. Device supply must be billed by furnishing entity; IDTFs excluded. Provider/QHP documentation required.

What is RTM?

What is CCM?
Chronic Care Management (CCM) supports patients with multiple chronic conditions requiring regular monitoring, medication management, and care coordination across providers. Services are structured and non-face-to-face with required documentation.
Patient Eligibility

- Significant risk of death, acute exacerbation, or functional decline

What is PCM?
Principal Care Management (PCM) is designed for Medicare patients with a single high-risk or complex chronic condition requiring monthly management. PCM codes support focused condition management and coordination.
Patient Eligibility
- Medicare beneficiaries with one high-risk chronic condition
- Condition must be expected to last at least 3+ months and place the patient at risk of hospitalization or functional decline.
Who May Bill?

What is TCM?
Transitional Care Management (TCM) supports the transition of Medicare patients from hospital/institutional care back to community. TCM addresses medication reconciliation, follow-up visits, and coordination within 7‚Äì14 days post-discharge.
Patient Eligibility

What is AWV?
The Medicare Annual Wellness Visit (AWV) is a yearly preventive visit that includes a structured health risk assessment, review of medical and family history, risk factor screening, and development or update of a personalized prevention plan. It is billed with G0438 (initial) and G0439 (subsequent) and may optionally include a billable SDOH risk assessment using G0136 under 2026 rules.
Patient Eligibility
- Medicare Part B beneficiary enrolled for ≥ 12 months
- Not within 12 months of an IPPE (G0402) or prior AWV (G0438/G0439)
- Can be combined with an optional, standardized SDOH risk assessment when clinically indicated.
Who May Bill?
Physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, clinical nurse specialists, and other practitioners allowed by CMS may bill AWV. Clinical staff may complete portions of the risk assessment and screening under appropriate supervision, with the billing practitioner reviewing and finalizing documentation.
Billing Structure
- G0438: Initial AWV with personalized prevention plan
- G0439: Subsequent AWV, annually after initial
- G0136: Optional SDOH risk assessment, 5‚Äì15 minutes, when furnished in conjunction with AWV or other qualifying E/M/behavioral health services (no more often than every 6 months).

What is SDOH Risk Assessment?
The Social Determinants of Health (SDOH) Risk Assessment is a brief, standardized questionnaire that identifies health-related social needs such as food, housing, transportation, utilities, and safety. Medicare pays separately for this service under HCPCS G0136 when a validated, evidence-based tool is used and results are documented and acted upon.
Patient Eligibility
- Medicare beneficiaries in whom social needs may affect diagnosis, treatment, or adherence
- Outpatient or other qualifying settings when furnished with an E/M, behavioral health service, or AWV
- Assessment not performed more than once every 6 months per practitioner per beneficiary.
Who May Bill?
Physicians and other practitioners eligible to bill Medicare Part B may bill G0136 when they or their supervised clinical staff administer the assessment. Clinical staff (e.g., RN, MA, social worker, care manager) can deliver the tool under incident-to or applicable supervision rules, with the billing practitioner reviewing and incorporating results into the care plan.
Billing Structure
- G0136: Administration of standardized, evidence-based SDOH risk assessment, 5‚Äì15 minutes
- May be billed with AWV (G0438/G0439), E/M, or behavioral health visits when criteria are met
- Not more often than every 6 months per practitioner per beneficiary.
Workflow
1
Screening
Administer PRAPARE, AHC-HRSN, or similar 5-domain tool (5‚Äì15 min).
2
Identify Needs
Flag positive findings (food, housing, transport, utilities, safety).
3
Referrals
Connect to community resources, social work, or APCM/CCM team.
4
Follow-up & Re-screen
Track outcomes, close-loop referrals, repeat every 6‚Äì12 months as needed.

What is GDMT?
Guideline-directed medical therapy (GDMT) for heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF) is a structured, evidence-based medication regimen built on four foundational drug classes that reduce mortality, HF hospitalizations, and improve quality of life. It combines renin‚Äìangiotensin system inhibition (ACEI/ARB/ARNI), evidence-based beta-blockers, mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists (MRA), and SGLT2 inhibitors, titrated to guideline doses whenever possible.
Patient Eligibility
- Symptomatic HFrEF (LVEF ‚â§ 40%) without contraindications to core drug classes.
- Often extended to high-risk HF patients with recent decompensation, incomplete GDMT, or poor adherence.
- HFmrEF/HFpEF patients may benefit from selected components (especially SGLT2 inhibitors) based on guideline and local protocol.
- Ideal candidates for a GDMT Enablement Program are those with recurrent hospitalizations, complex regimens, or difficulty maintaining adherence.
Who May Manage?
GDMT initiation and titration are typically led by cardiologists, HF specialists, or primary care clinicians, often supported by clinical pharmacists and advanced practice providers. Remote/digital optimization programs use nurse care managers and HF titration teams who review RPM data and adherence signals, then escalate dose changes and medication adjustments to prescribing clinicians within protocol-driven pathways.
Clinical Components
- Confirm HF phenotype and EF; review current medications and contraindications.
- Initiate or optimize each GDMT ‚Äúpillar‚Äù: ACEI/ARB/ARNI, beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2 inhibitor, plus diuretic strategy and other indicated therapies (e.g., hydralazine/isosorbide in select patients).
- Monitor vitals (BP, HR), weight, renal function, electrolytes, and symptoms for tolerance and decompensation.
- Use titration schedules and protocolized pathways to reach target or maximally tolerated doses over several weeks to months.
Devices & RPM Integration
LucidAct‚Äôs GDMT Enablement Program leverages a connected device kit for HF patients, typically including:

- Blood pressure monitor (for safe up-titration of vasodilators and beta-blockers).
- Weight scale (for volume status and early HF decompensation signals).

- Smart pillbox (for real-time adherence monitoring and reminders).

Daily device data feeds RPM dashboards and predictive alerts so care teams can identify emerging issues (weight/BP shifts, missed doses, worsening symptoms) and intervene early.
Key Benefits
Clinical Outcomes
- Higher rates of complete GDMT implementation and titration to guideline doses.
- Fewer HF-related hospitalizations and ED visits; improved survival and functional status.
- Earlier detection of decompensation through weight/BP/symptom trends.

Operational Efficiency
- Daily data streams and actionable alerts focus staff on patients with real risk signals.
- Integrated documentation and templated titration visits reduce manual charting.
- Epic/Redox integration enables two-way data exchange and minimizes double entry.

Financial Impact
- Reduced total cost of care and readmission penalties for value-based contracts.
- Incremental fee-for-service revenue via RPM, CCM, and associated care management codes.
- Improved performance on HF quality metrics tied to medication use and outcomes.

- Enroll into the GDMT Enablement Program and ship connected device kit (BP, scale, pulse ox, smart pillbox).
